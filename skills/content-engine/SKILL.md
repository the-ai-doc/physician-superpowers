# Content Engine Skill

The physician content creator grind: you have expertise that could help thousands. But filming, editing, captioning, scheduling — it's a second job.

This skill takes **one source** (a lecture recording, article, clinical pearl, voice note) and produces formatted content for every platform you're active on.

---

## Usage

Say: **"content engine"**

Then provide one of:
- A YouTube video URL or transcript
- A voice note transcript
- A written article or blog post
- A key clinical insight (even just a sentence or two)

Or more specifically:
- *"content engine — here's my lecture transcript: [paste]"*
- *"content engine — repurpose this article for LinkedIn and Instagram"*
- *"content engine — I want to post about [topic], generate a week of content"*

---

## What It Produces

From a single source, the skill generates:

| Platform | Format | Length |
|---|---|---|
| LinkedIn | Long-form professional post | 800–1200 words |
| Twitter/X | Thread (8–12 tweets) | Punchy, quotable |
| Instagram | Carousel caption + 5–7 slide outlines | Visual-first |
| TikTok/Reels | Hook + script outline | 30–60 seconds |
| Newsletter | Email-ready section | 300–400 words |
| YouTube (short) | Script for 60-second short | Direct, educational |

---

## Instructions for the Agent

When triggered:

**STEP 1 — Understand the source:**
Ask the user to provide or paste the source content. If it's a URL, use `read_url_content` to fetch it.

**STEP 2 — Extract the core insight:**
Identify:
- The main clinical or educational point
- The target audience (patients? physicians? general public?)
- The physician's unique angle or personal perspective
- Any data, stats, or stories that make it memorable

**STEP 3 — Generate platform content:**

For each platform, follow the format below:

**LinkedIn:**
- Hook line (stops the scroll)
- Personal story or clinical context
- The insight, clearly explained
- Practical takeaway
- CTA: "Follow for more" or question for engagement
- 4–5 relevant hashtags (#PhysicianLife #HealthcareAI #MedTwitter etc.)

**Twitter/X Thread:**
- Tweet 1: Bold hook or controversial take
- Tweets 2–10: One idea per tweet, building logically
- Tweet 11: Summary + CTA

**Instagram Carousel:**
- Slide 1: Hook (designed to stop scroll)
- Slides 2–6: One point per slide, visual-first language
- Slide 7: CTA ("Save this" / "Share with a colleague")
- Caption: Short, emoji-forward, 3–5 hashtags

**TikTok/Reels Script:**
- Hook (first 3 seconds — must grab attention immediately)
- Middle: The content, fast-paced
- End: CTA or cliffhanger

**Newsletter:**
- Subject line (3 options)
- 300-word section ready to paste into ConvertKit/Beehiiv/Substack

**STEP 4 — Output:**
Present content in clearly labeled sections, formatted and ready to copy-paste. No editing needed — post-ready.

**STEP 5 — Blotato handoff (if applicable):**
If the user has Blotato set up, note that they can paste the content into Blotato for:
- AI image/visual generation
- Scheduling across platforms
- Analytics on performance

## HIPAA Reminder ⚠️
**Never include:** real patient identifying information, specific case details that could identify a patient, or protected health information of any kind. The skill will flag if source content appears to contain PHI.
